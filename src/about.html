The Beginning

A Restaurant Born from Necessity & Love

Oaks Restaurant was born in 2018 from a simple but powerful idea: that Nigerian cuisine deserved a stage worthy of its complexity. Chef Nnamdi Okonkwo, raised between his grandmother's compound kitchen in Awka and the fine dining restaurants of London, returned home with a mission.

"I was tired of watching our food be treated as an afterthought," he says. "Egusi deserves the same reverence as bouillabaisse. Suya has the same depth as any great barbecue tradition in the world."

What We Stand For

Our Values

Provenance First

We source from named farms and markets. Our customers know where their food comes from — and so do we.

Fire as Technique

We cook over hardwood. Not gas, not electric. The smoke, the char — these are flavours, not accidents.

Heritage Without Nostalgia

We respect the past but refuse to be frozen in it. Nigerian cuisine is alive and evolving. We evolve with it.

Radical Hospitality

Every guest is family. We don't do cold professionalism. We do warmth, laughter, and service that makes you return.
